The mascot is an anime mercenary in a gold-filigree kimono, dual-wielding pistols, standing in a hail of flying Swiss over a torii gate. That is not a mistake. That is the thesis.

I build defensive systems. I think in assume-breach, in layered defense, in deception and rollback timers and the cold question of *what happens when this fails and I am not watching*. That work is serious. It demands precision. It does not demand that I be a tedious person about it — and somewhere along the way the security industry confused the two.

So: Cheese Violence. A worked heel. A villain who's in on the joke. The kimono is the swagger; the cheese is the wink that keeps the swagger from curdling into cringe. If you came expecting a thought-leader in a quarter-zip telling you to *leverage synergies*, you took a wrong turn at the torii gate. Go back. The gun is loaded with dairy.

## What this is

A personal zine. 個人誌. Vol. 002 of a thing whose Vol. 001 was a single merch run — one drop, no restock, gone when it was gone. The blog inherits that ethos.

I don't publish on a calendar. I publish when a piece is *good*. A dispatch ships because it earned the slot, not because it's Tuesday and the content machine is hungry. Most of what gets written here never gets posted. What survives is what was true, sharp, and worth your time — and once it ships, it ships finished. One run. No restock. I'm not coming back to edit it into something safer next quarter.

That constraint is the whole point. Scarcity forces quality. A blog that posts daily to feed an algorithm is a blog that stopped having anything to say and started having an obligation to fill. I have enough obligations. This isn't one of them.

## What this is not

It's not a tutorial mill. It's not my actual network — you get principles, never my blueprint, because publishing your real topology under your real name is how amateurs get owned, and I'm not an amateur. It's not a diary with dates and names and the texture of a life I'd rather not hand to strangers. Stay evergreen, stay reflective, stay useful.

Think of it as field notes from someone who does the work and has opinions about how it should be done. Confidence, not hedging. Findings, not vibes.

> The persona is theatrical. The substance is not. That contract holds every single dispatch.

## The territory

No menu. A menu is a promise, and promises age badly. But here's the shape of the ground.

I live in the place where a firewall rule and a brew schedule are the same kind of problem: a system, a set of constraints, a failure mode, and a design that fails gracefully when it hits one. I'll write about defending things and breaking them — the purple-team view, where the only way to trust your defense is to be the one attacking it. I'll write about the homelab as a discipline: infrastructure built to survive being moved, unplugged, and generally betrayed by circumstance.

And then the rest of it. The homebrew, because fermentation is just a slow incident you're trying to control. The decks. The learning, because I'm always three tracks deep in something I don't yet understand, and the not-understanding is the fun part.

Different surfaces. Same operator. Same move every time: take it apart, find the failure mode, write the operating manual, ship it when it's right.

That's the brand. That's the why.

Vol. 003 will be better than this one. It has to be. That's the only rule.

— *San Francisco / Los Angeles / Nowhere*
