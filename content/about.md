Before the costume, there's a person who builds walls for a living and can't leave a system alone until he understands why it holds. The swagger you see on the way in — the gunfighter pose, the dairy weaponized into a calling card — is a costume that person put on for a reason. That's the mask. This is the face.

I build defenses for a living. Cybersecurity architect, homelab operator, purple-team practitioner — which is a fancy way of saying I spend my days playing both the attacker and the wall, and I take both jobs personally. I run a cyber range out of a closet that has no business being as hardened as it is. I think in assume-breach. I trust nothing by default and I document everything, because documentation is the operating manual for the version of you that shows up at 2 a.m. with no memory and a problem.

That's the day job. The obsessions don't stop there.

I brew beer and chase the gravity numbers like they owe me money. I DJ. I run a 3D printer hot enough to print my own organizers for the gear the other hobbies generate. I'm always mid-way through some learning track — networking, cloud, Python — never one at a time, always three, because a single discipline feels like leaving range on the table. The thread connecting all of it is the same: take a system apart, understand why it works, rebuild it better, write down how.

> Cheese Violence is the heel turn. The villain who's in on the joke.

So here's the quiet part, said plainly. This whole thing is a worked heel. In pro wrestling, a heel is the villain — and a *worked* heel is one where everybody, the crowd included, knows the menace is theater. The swagger is real. The threat is a bit. The cheese is, regrettably, load-bearing.

Why the mask? Because the work itself is careful, layered, and allergic to hype. It's rollback-first and confidence-rated and deeply unglamorous. The mascot gets to be everything the work can't: loud, ridiculous, a little mythic. He carries the noir so the notes can stay honest.

Here's what to expect. Field notes from someone who does the work — how I think about defense, deception, resilient infrastructure, and building things that survive being moved. Some brewing. Some signal. Strong opinions, priority-ranked, confidence attached. No fluff, no front, no restock.

This is Vol. 002 — the 個人誌, the personal zine to the merch's Vol. 001.

San Francisco. Los Angeles. Nowhere. One run.

Pull up a chair. The cheese is already in the air — you might as well see where it lands.
